Mid to Late 1800s

The Near extinction of surfing

Top to bottom: One of Hawaii's sugar plantations, American missionaries and native Hawaiians

In the 1700s and the early 1800s surfing was a favourite pastime where Hawaiians would drop everything to go surfing. By the mid 1800s American missionaries had moved in where surfing was frowned upon as it was time consuming and dangerous. In a journal written by a missionary, Hawaiians were described as

“chattering and almost naked savages,”.

Churches were built, sermons were given and the bible was translated into Hawaiian. The cash economy was introduced and Hawaii became industrialised. With the loss of their spare time mainly due to them working in sugar cane fields and worst of all diseases such as syphilis which the westerners brought in possibly declined the Hawaiian population to about 10 percent and thus the near extinction
