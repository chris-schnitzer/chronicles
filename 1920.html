Surfboard evolution

Top: A traditional Hawaiian Puua. Bottom: The newer Cigar shape.

The surfboard in the top image is the traditional Puua shape made out of redwood which has been used up until about the 1920s. The shape is longer and squarer with parrallel rails.

The board in the bottom image is one of Tom Blakes cigar shapes which became hugely popular in the 1920s. It is shorter, more oval and has a sharper tail. These designs allow for more speed and maneuverabilty.
